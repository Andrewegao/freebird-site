Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 20, 2026 · Effective: May 20, 2026

Freebird is built around a simple promise: the personal things stay on your phone. There is no account, no sign-in, and no email to hand over. This policy explains exactly what stays on your device, the small amount of anonymous data that leaves it, and the choices you control. It applies to the Freebird iPhone app and this website (together, the "Service"), operated by Freebird ("we," "us").

The short version. Your birthday, interests, saved items, ZIP, and value ledger never leave your device. We have no accounts, so nothing is tied to your identity. The only data that leaves your phone is anonymous: product analytics, an opt-in push token with coarse topics, and the ad and measurement data the AdMob SDK requires — and the advertising identifier is read only if you allow it through Apple's App Tracking Transparency prompt.

1. What stays on your device

The following are stored only on your iPhone (in local app storage) and are never uploaded to us or anyone else. They are not part of any account, because Freebird has no accounts.

  • Your birthday (and optional birth year) — used only to schedule local reminders on your device before birthday sign-up deadlines.
  • Your interests — used only to order your feed locally.
  • Your home ZIP code — used only to surface nearby offers locally; we do not collect precise location.
  • Your saved items and your value ledger (what you have claimed and its approximate value).

Because these never leave your phone, they are not included in our App Store privacy label as collected data. If you delete the app, this on-device data is removed with it.

2. What leaves your device

The only data that leaves your phone is anonymous — it is not tied to your name, email, account, or precise location, because we collect none of those.

Anonymous product analytics

We record a small, fixed set of anonymous events to understand which parts of the app are useful and to improve curation and ranking — for example, app opens, which feed sections are viewed, saves, claim taps, ad impressions, and reports of expired or wrong offers. These pings carry only stable content identifiers and a closed set of enumerated values, plus coarse context such as app version and iOS major version. Each app launch generates a random, ephemeral session id that is discarded when the app closes and is never reused or persisted.

We deliberately do not log: your birthday or anything derived from it (not the date, the month, or "days until birthday"); your name, email, phone, or any account id (none exist); precise location; or any free text such as search queries or typed notes.

Push notification token

If you opt in to notifications, your device registers an anonymous push token with Apple Push Notification service (via Firebase Cloud Messaging) so we can send the weekly digest and free-day reminders you asked for. The token carries no personal information and no birthday — only coarse, opt-in topics such as a content category, a coarse region, or a free-day. You can turn notifications off at any time in iOS Settings.

Advertising and measurement data

Freebird is free and supported by clearly labeled ads served through Google AdMob (and any ad mediation networks AdMob uses). The AdMob SDK collects standard ad-serving and measurement data to show ads, cap how often you see them, and measure ad performance. See section 3 for how the advertising identifier is gated, and section 6 for the third parties involved.

3. Advertising and App Tracking Transparency

Apple's App Tracking Transparency (ATT) framework controls whether an app may read your device's advertising identifier (IDFA) for cross-app tracking. Freebird shows the ATT prompt before any advertising identifier is read.

  • If you allow tracking: AdMob may read your advertising identifier and use it for personalized third-party advertising and ad measurement.
  • If you do not allow tracking: no advertising identifier is read, and ads fall back to non-personalized. The app works exactly the same.

Our own analytics pings never contain the advertising identifier — that stays inside the ad SDK. You can change your choice at any time in iOS Settings under Privacy & Security → Tracking.

4. No accounts, no identity

Freebird has no sign-in, no email, no password, and no user profile. We do not collect your name, email address, phone number, or any account identifier, and we do not build a profile that could re-identify you. Under Apple's framework, this means no data is "linked to you," because there is no identity to link it to.

5. How we use anonymous data

We use the anonymous data above only to:

  • Keep the feed current and improve curation and ranking;
  • Send the notifications you opt in to (weekly digest, free-day reminders);
  • Process reports of expired or incorrect offers through a human-review queue;
  • Serve and measure the clearly labeled ads that keep Freebird free.

We measure in order to turn notification frequency down and make content more relevant — never to justify spam or to profile you.

7. Notifications

Notifications are entirely opt-in. Birthday reminders are scheduled locally on your device from the birthday you set, so they work without sending your birthday anywhere. Topic-based notifications (weekly digest, free-day reminders) use the anonymous push token described above. Turn them off any time in iOS Settings.

8. Retention

On-device data stays until you change or delete it, or delete the app. Anonymous analytics pings are retained in aggregate only and contain no identity. Provider-held data (e.g. AdMob ad data, host server logs) is retained per each provider's policy.

9. Children

Freebird is rated 4+ and is intended for a general audience. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children, and the app collects no personal data from anyone. If you believe a child has provided personal data to us, contact us and we will address it.

10. Your choices and rights

  • Tracking: allow or deny the advertising identifier via the ATT prompt, or change it later in iOS Settings → Privacy & Security → Tracking.
  • Notifications: opt in or out in iOS Settings.
  • On-device data: edit your birthday, interests, ZIP, and saved items in the app at any time, or delete the app to remove all on-device data.
  • Analytics: because pings are anonymous and carry no identifier, there is no profile to access, correct, or delete. Deleting the app stops all future pings.

Depending on where you live, you may have rights under laws such as the GDPR or the CCPA/CPRA. Because we hold no data tied to your identity, most such requests are satisfied by the controls above. You may still contact us with any privacy question (section 13).

11. Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information (California)

Under the California Consumer Privacy Act as amended by the CPRA, the use of advertising identifiers for cross-context behavioral advertising may be treated as a "sale" or "share." Freebird does not sell or share any personal information except, potentially, the advertising identifier used by AdMob for personalized ads — and that identifier is read only if you grant permission through Apple's App Tracking Transparency prompt.

To opt out of any such sharing, simply decline the ATT prompt, or turn tracking off at any time in iOS Settings → Privacy & Security → Tracking. When tracking is off, no advertising identifier is read or shared and ads are non-personalized. Everything personal — your birthday, interests, ZIP, saved items, and ledger — already stays on your device and is never sold or shared.

12. Changes to this policy

If we change this policy, we will update the "Last updated" date above and, for material changes, note them in the app or on this page. Continued use after an update means you accept the revised policy.

13. Contact

Because Freebird has no accounts and collects no personal data tied to your identity, you can manage everything yourself from within the app and iOS Settings, as described above. For privacy questions, you can reach us through the support channel listed on the app's App Store page.
